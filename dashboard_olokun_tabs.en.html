MVP Pilot: Starting Small

Realistic approach to validate methodology before scaling

Philosophy: Feet on the Ground

Before thinking about 995 schools, let's validate the methodology with a pilot group of 10-15 schools in Florianópolis. Focus on learning, adjustments and measurable results.

School Selection Criteria

  • Accessibility: Central location in Florianópolis
  • Engaged management: Directors receptive to innovation
  • Type diversity: Public (Federal/State/Municipal) + Private
  • Varied size: 50-200 students per school
  • Basic infrastructure: Space for activities and meetings

What will be validated in the Pilot

  • Methodology: Effectiveness of workshops and materials
  • Engagement: Adoption rate of teachers and students
  • Logistics: Operational viability and real costs
  • Impact: Measurable behavior change
  • Barriers: Identification of unforeseen obstacles

Detailed Pilot Timeline

Weeks 1-2: Preparation & Diagnosis
  • Selection and confirmation of 10-15 pilot schools
  • Individual meetings with school managers
  • Initial baseline questionnaire application with teachers
  • Infrastructure and available resources survey
  • Executing team formation (2-3 people)
Weeks 3-6: Teacher Training
  • 4 weekly 3-hour workshops (waste management, circular economy, coastal education)
  • Delivery of pedagogical kit (booklets, posters, teaching materials)
  • Co-creation of adapted lesson plans for local reality
  • WhatsApp group formation for continuous support
Weeks 7-14: School Implementation
  • Teachers apply activities in classroom (2-3 classes per week)
  • Visual campaigns in schools (environment week, etc)
  • Follow-up visits by Olokun team every two weeks
  • Collection of qualitative data (observations, photos, reports)
  • Real-time methodology adjustments
Weeks 15-20: Impact Assessment
  • Final endline questionnaire application with teachers and students
  • Focus groups with teachers for qualitative feedback
  • Behavior change analysis (before vs after)
  • Real costs and invested time survey
  • Documentation of lessons learned and best practices
Weeks 21-24: Report & Decision
  • Preparation of final report with quantitative indicators
  • Presentation of results to managers and potential partners
  • Decision: Scale, Adjust or Pivot?
  • If validated: preparation of replication kit for Phase 2
  • If not validated: refinement and new pilot cycle

Strengths vs Challenges

Strategic analysis for Olokun Institute implementation

Strengths

  • Consolidated road infrastructure in Florianópolis
  • Access to established education networks
  • High-quality geospatial data available
  • School concentration in urban areas
  • Identified government partners
  • Communities with mobilization history
  • School clusters by proximity

Challenges

  • Dispersion of private schools in peripheries
  • Lack of infrastructure in rural areas
  • Budget limitations for mass training
  • Long distances between municipalities
  • Resource heterogeneity between schools
  • Incomplete data on coastal areas
  • Resistance to initiatives not aligned with curriculum

Strategic Insight

The concentration of schools in Florianópolis (capital) offers opportunity for high-impact pilots, while distribution in Greater Florianópolis allows gradual and sustainable territorial expansion.

Long-Term Vision

If the pilot validates the methodology, this is the path for gradual expansion

⚠️ Important

This expansion plan will only be activated after successful pilot validation. The numbers and timelines may change based on lessons learned.

PHASE 1
Pilot

Florianópolis - Initial Expansion

Duration: 6-12 months (after pilot)

Goal: Replicate validated methodology to 30-50 schools

Target schools: 25 public + 10 private in urban neighborhoods

Main actions:

  • Selection of anchor schools with committed managers
  • Intensive training of teacher multipliers
  • Focused coastal awareness campaigns
  • Collection of feedback and methodology adjustments
KPI: 90% adhesion and initial engagement
PHASE 2
Scale

Greater Florianópolis - Regional Scale

Duration: 12-24 months

Goal: Expand to 100-150 schools in neighboring municipalities

Target schools: 80 public + 30 private in 8-10 municipalities

Main actions:

  • Replication of methodology validated in pilot
  • Partnerships with municipal education secretariats
  • Formation of local multiplier nuclei
  • Regional thematic campaigns (coastal, marine)
KPI: 70% coverage in phase 2
PHASE 3
Consolidation

Complete Territorial Consolidation

Duration: 24-36 months

Goal: Integration into public policies and sustainability

Target schools: 300+ schools (significant regional coverage)

Main actions:

  • Integration into official networks curriculum
  • Certification of teachers and multipliers
  • Financial sustainability via public-private partnerships
  • Continuous environmental impact monitoring
KPI: 95% coverage and measurable impact
